ERNAKULAM






At a Glance

Ernakulam, the urbane face of Kochi, is a bustling place with crowded shopping malls, restaurants and high-rises. On the cultural, political, commercial and industrial fronts, Ernakulam is on the cutting edge, as far as the State is concerned.With the Arabian Sea on one side and the flourishing cities like Thrissur, Alappuzha, and Kottayam on the other, Eranakulam enjoys a position of being the most commercially rich cities of Kerala. No doubt this place is known as the Commercial Capital of Kerala, all because it hosts the highest number of International and domestic tourists and is the major business hub of Kerala with the highest revenue yielding record in the state.here is so much to see and explore in Ernakulam, it has various historical sites, temples, forts, beaches, museums, and other tourist places to attract travelers from all around the world. No doubt that Ernakulam is an ultimate destination for a globetrotter, who aims to explore as much as they can in their single visit to 'God's Own Country.

Activities

Cochin Carnival is a carnival held every year in the last week of December at Fort Kochi in the city of Kochi, Kerala.



Explore


Fort Kochi

Set foot into Fort Kochi and you will be instantly transported to a different time period. This place is steeped in the history and culture of all who have occupied it through the ages. Its roots and essence are unique in its diversity. The streets, shops and the residents operate at their own pace and visitors will feel instantly transported into another era altogether.

Thattekad bird sanctuary

Located near Kothamangalam in Ernakulam on the northern bank of Periyar River, Thattekad Bird Sanctuary is famous for its rich and varied birdlife. It is the first Bird Sanctuary in Kerala. While you traverse the dense forests containing tropical semi-evergreen, deciduous and tropical evergreen plants, do not miss out on the boat cruise along the backwaters.

Bhoothathankettu

Located about 10 km from Kodamangalam.Bhoothathankettu is a dam that also doubles as a tourism spot attracting a large number of people around the year with its picturesque views, bountiful nature. The Malayalam name Bhoothathankettu means, 'monster fort', which throws light to that legend pervading the crude dam built by the demons.
