IDUKKI






At a Glance

Idukki is Kerala's second largest district and has been blessed with numerous natural wonders. The high valleys and hill ranges of this landlocked region are fed by three major rivers - Periyar, Thalayar and Thodupuzhayar and their tributaries. Its forest still hosts many tribes and over half of the terrain is still covered with forests. People love going to the gigantic arch dam for getting its mesmerising view.Mist laden hills, enchanting valleys, gushing streams, cascades, rolling grasslands, sprawling plantations, colonial bungalows, wildlife and many more makes Idukki, a landlocked district in Central Kerala a unique destination for travellers. The hilly terrain, which has the highest point in South India- Anaimudi offers ample scope for trekking, other adventures sports. It is one of the districts in Kerala with a predominant tribal population. With the famous hill station Munnar as its crowning glory, Idukki also has numerous other places of interest that adds to the destination value of the district.


Seasons

The winter season is considered the season to explore Idukki.


Location

The altitude of the place is 1,200 meters or 3,900 feet above sea surface.

Cuisine

Munnar is the natural hub of homemade chocolates. Anyone with sweet tooth must tast this.

Activities

Neelakurinji that blooms once in every 12 years is a major attraction here.



Explore


Eravikulam National park

Located in the high ranges of Kerala which offers the magnificent view of hills with tea plantations - the Eravikulam National Park. Eravikulam is also famous for the natural habitat of Nilgiri Tahr- endangered goat and this park is built with the aim of conserving them. Every twelve years, this is one of the places where you can witness the mass flowering of Neelakurinji flowers.

Periyar wildlife santuary

Saddled in the southern region of the Western Ghats, spread across an area of 777 sq. km of forest land and home to a luxuriant collection of different species of flora and fauna is the Periyar Tiger Reserve. This spectacular stretch of greenery gets its name from the River Periyar which has its origin deep inside the reserve. The reserve also offers eco-tourism programmes to the travellers.

Munnar

Munnar rises as three mountain streams merge, 1,600 m above sea level.One of the most sought after destinations in Kerala. Munnar is replete with resorts and logding facilities that fit a wide rage of budgets. Sprawling tea plantations, picturesque towns, winding lanes and holiday facilities make this a popular resort town. Munnar also has the highest peak in South India, Anamudi.
